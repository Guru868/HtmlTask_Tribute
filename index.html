About A.P.J Abdul Kalam

A.P.J Abdul Kalam, known as the " A.P.J. Abdul Kalam", the " Man of India," was born on October 15, 1931, in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He served as the 11th President of India and was a renowned scientist, known for his work in aerospace engineering and his pivotal role in India's civilian space program and military missile development.

Before his presidency, he worked with ISRO (Indian Space Research Organisation) and DRDO (Defence Research and Development Organisation), where he was instrumental in the development of several key projects, including the SLV-3, India's first satellite launch vehicle.
